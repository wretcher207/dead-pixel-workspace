## Understanding Claude AI: What makes it different?

![[7e463617fb5e9767bd1a5eac3db5b232_MD5.png]]

screenshot of Claude AI website homepage with the words meet Claude, it is AI for all of us

Unlike other AI systems, Claude AI’s training model is based on a concept known as constitutional AI. Constitutional AI is a training method for [AI models](https://www.jotform.com/ai/ai-models/), especially large language models, which lays out a set of rules and guidelines designed to better align with human values. Claude, in particular, uses a constitution based on the UN Declaration of Human Rights, trust and safety best practices, and other ethical guidelines.

These rules are in place to generate answers that are considered helpful, honest, and harmless. Using its constitution, [Claude AI](https://claude.ai/) models can self-critique their responses. This reduces the need for extensive human oversight during training and automates response refinement over time.

Claude prompt engineering must therefore be clear and aligned with Claude’s constitution to generate content that’s both helpful and avoids harmful use, such as biased or offensive content. Other models, like OpenAI’s ChatGPT, may offer a broader range of prompts and responses, but their answers can be influenced by online bias and lack Claude’s in-depth analysis.

These differences make Claude an ideal model for things like

- Long-context understanding
- Nuanced analysis
- Code generation
- In-depth writing
- Complex problem solving

Like many other AI models, there are multiple iterations of this technology. Anthropic currently offers three core versions of Claude, including

- **Claude Opus:** The most powerful of the three versions, Claude Opus is used for complex tasks and reasoning.
- **Claude Sonnet:** Sonnet is the middle option, focused on balance. It offers a mix of speed and complexity, which is often best suited for enterprise applications.
- **Claude Haiku:** This streamlined model is all about speed. Haiku is a compact model that’s designed to produce quick responses, though it sacrifices a high level of capabilities.

Now that you’re aware of the nuances of the Claude AI system, you can understand why it’s important to know exactly what prompts will and won’t be effective. The right prompts ultimately make this tool more useful because of its foundation in constitutional AI.

## The building blocks of effective Claude prompting

When crafting the ideal Claude prompt, we recommend considering a few key factors. Using these guidelines, you can form effective prompts that produce optimal results.

- **Clarity and directness:** Specificity is key with Claude. Being clear about what exactly you’re looking for will help guide your AI model to the correct answer. Rather than “write me a blog post,” try specifying the length, tone, and topic.
- **Context:** Offer Claude any relevant information necessary to guide its generation. Details such as desired outcome, background, and any exact needs are essential for getting the response you want.
- **Structured communication:** Claude is designed to think like a human, so consider how you would approach the same task with a colleague. Offer a clear, logical explanation for what you’re asking. And if a prompt seems too confusing, split it into individual steps using a [prompt chaining](https://www.jotform.com/ai/prompt-chaining/) framework.

These principles can lay the groundwork for a strong approach to Claude prompt engineering. By breaking down how to build a single Claude prompt, you’ll better understand how to apply these tactics to other prompts in real time.

## Step-by-step guide to prompting Claude

Just like with any conversation, the way you phrase a question or command is essential to its outcome. Building a Claude prompt is no different. Follow these steps to ensure yours is well received by your AI model and produces the results you not only want, but expect from an advanced solution like Claude.

### 1\. Structure your prompt

The way your prompt is presented will determine how Claude approaches its answer. High-level, vague prompts require Claude to do much of the heavy lifting and could lead to results that don’t align with your expectations.

To keep Claude on track, focus on specificity and structure. If you have a large task, separate it into smaller tasks. Claude will have an easier time handling multiple smaller tasks accurately than one large task with many moving parts.

For a more advanced method of prompting Claude, you can include XML tags like <text>, <examples>, or <instructions> to more clearly label and organize data in a language that’s easier for the AI model to understand.

### 2\. Provide clear context and role assignment

Including what may seem like extra information can be the key to effective Claude prompt engineering. Background information offers a perspective that can be useful for Claude as it builds out responses.

For example, when asking Claude to write something, clarify who’s doing the writing. A creative writer may approach a blog differently than a technical writer, making this distinction a particularly important factor in your final result.

### 3\. Use examples and demonstrations

Just like a real human being, having access to examples of similar work can streamline the learning process and produce better results from your Claude prompt. The use of examples becomes increasingly important the more complex your task is.

Consider including examples of both inputs and outputs so that Claude can better understand your expectations and craft responses to match. The more examples you provide, the more likely Claude is to match your needs.

### 4\. Optimize output format and style

Creating a consistent output format and style may take some intervention on your end. While Claude will naturally refine its results over time, you can accelerate this process by defining your desired output style using JSON, XML, or custom templates.

This level of specificity can help Claude understand every element of your desired outputs — and help you avoid frustrating mishaps.

## Common Claude prompting mistakes and how to avoid them

Once you get familiar with how to prompt Claude, you can expand the possibilities of its use. However, be careful not to fall victim to some of the most common prompting mistakes that could cause you to waste your time and effort.

### Vague prompt

**The problem:** Your Claude prompt lacks sufficient detail, forcing Claude to produce results that either fail to meet your needs or are ineffective. This results in wasted effort, lackluster outcomes, and rushed timelines.

**The solution:** Provide Claude with as much detail as possible about what exactly you’re looking for. Instead of asking it to write a social post about a new product, consider including further information on the channels where it should be shared, your target audience, and any other relevant information. Claude can use the additional guidance you provide to refine its results.

### Information overload

**The problem:** You’ve asked Claude to do too many tasks at once. Whether it be a large task with a multitude of moving parts or a string of tasks all meant to be done together, Claude will struggle to produce optimal results when overloaded. The output will likely be vague and low quality. Either your large task will be done to an incomplete degree, or all of your small tasks will be done ineffectively in an attempt to complete them all at once.

**The solution:** Focus your efforts on single, manageable tasks. Consider a Claude prompt engineering strategy that breaks down large tasks into a series of sequential steps. By allowing Claude to focus on one task at a time, you can more effectively produce quality results.

### Lack of context

**The problem:** Claude can’t read your mind — it can only create outputs based on the information you provide. While it can come to some conclusions on its own, leaving out essential guidelines can result in outcomes that don’t align with your intended voice, tone, or style.

**The solution:** Give Claude a clear picture of what you’re looking for in an output. Whether that be writing style, length, intended audience, or otherwise, all of this information can transform a generic result into a refined masterpiece. If you’re looking for Claude to create something similar to another sample, provide the relevant example pieces or demonstrations as inspiration.

### Ignoring Claude’s limitations

**The problem:** Although Claude may seem capable of anything, this solution has its limits — even in comparison to [other AI models](https://www.jotform.com/ai/best-ai-models/) like ChatGPT. If you ask Claude to perform tasks it’s incapable of doing, such as real-time web browsing or testing code, you may be met with results that are both suboptimal and frustrating.

**The solution:** Read up on what Claude is best used for and how the system works overall. Understanding limitations such as token limits, knowledge cutoffs, and safety constraints can help you better navigate its use. To get the most out of Claude, you must work within the boundaries of its limitations and find other ways to complete the kind of tasks it’s not best equipped for.

Knowing how to avoid these issues is key to effective Claude prompt engineering. But even the best prompt writers slip up every once in a while. Want some extra help? Consider adding a smart companion to your toolbox to help you stay on track when crafting a Claude prompt.
