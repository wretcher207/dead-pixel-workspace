# Meeting Annie

**Source:** `raw/notes/MeetingAnnie.docx`
**Format:** Narrative essay / chapter draft

## Summary

How David and [[annie]] met. A few months after his wife left him. He wasn't looking for anything. She followed him on Instagram, commented on his weed posts, and next thing he knows they're having sex by the railroad tracks next to the town waste management facility. She seemed okay with who he was — the bong rip videos, the scary music, the dark humor. That was new for him. She was good at sensing when he was pulling away and would pull him back.

Then it all came apart. Infidelity, manipulation, the pattern that defines the [[annie]] throughline in the larger [[writing-project]].

## Key Themes

- **Vulnerability after divorce** — David was raw, trying to be alone, not ready. Annie found the crack.
- **The good part was real** — He acknowledges she was genuinely good at reassurance early on. She played the songs that calm him down, gave him space. This isn't a one-dimensional villain story.
- **Self-awareness as identity** — David tried to warn her off ("you don't know me, dotty. I'm a rebel, dotty"). He knows he's a lot. She said "that's okay" and that disarmed him.
- **The pivot** — From "she was cool though" to "this bitch was evil" in the space of a paragraph. The writing mirrors how fast it actually happened.

## Notable Details

- They met on Instagram
- First encounter: by the railroad tracks, adjacent to the town waste management facility, in her gray Jeep Renegade
- David's favorite podcast at the time: Cumtown
- The music/bong rip video hobby — "spend roughly an hour or more setting up the shot"
