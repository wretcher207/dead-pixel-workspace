# Annie Doesn't Drink

**Source:** `raw/notes/AnnieDoesntDrink.txt.docx`
**Format:** Narrative essay / chapter draft

## Summary

A single-scene piece about David discovering that [[annie]] has been secretly drinking — tequila and strawberry mixer hidden in a breast pump bottle by the bathtub. She denies it through multiple rounds of confrontation despite overwhelming evidence. David catches her through smell, taste, behavior, and finally finding the bottle. The piece ends with a darkly comic moment: David drinking half the bottle himself while she pretends she's never seen it before.

## Key Themes

- **Die for the lie** — Annie's defining behavioral pattern. She will never admit to anything, no matter how obvious the evidence. David names this explicitly.
- **Pattern recognition** — David clocks every detail: the smell of fermented strawberry kool-aid, one eye half-focused, 13 bathroom trips in 30 minutes, holding her breath during sex. He's hypervigilant because he's been burned.
- **The irony layer** — Annie's drink of choice is the exact cocktail her hated ex-mother-in-law drinks. Annie is also short, blonde, blue-eyed. David sees the irony and names it.
- **[[rory]] connection** — Annie blames Rory for the beer bottles. The deflection pattern mirrors what David describes in the character sheet.

## Writing Craft Notes

This is the most fully-realized scene in the source collection. It has:
- A clear narrative arc (suspicion → confrontation → denial → discovery → punchline)
- Sustained tension with comedic release
- Physical detail that grounds every moment
- A punchline ending that's both funny and sad
