# MCP Explained Like You're a Business Owner, Not a Developer

You keep hearing the letters MCP. Maybe in an article about AI. Maybe from someone trying to sell you something. Nobody seems to explain what it means in a way that connects to your actual business. Let me fix that.

## The Simple Version

MCP stands for Model Context Protocol. Ignore the name. Here's what it does: it lets Claude connect to the tools you already use.

Without MCP, Claude is a brain in a jar. It can think, write, and analyze, but it can't touch anything in your world. It can't read your email. It can't check your calendar. It can't pull data from your CRM. You have to copy and paste everything back and forth like it's 2009.

With MCP, Claude plugs directly into your tools. Gmail, Google Calendar, Slack, Notion, HubSpot, Asana. The list is growing fast. And through Zapier integration, you can connect it to over 8,000 apps.

Think of MCP like USB for AI. A standard plug that lets Claude talk to whatever you're already using. You don't need to know how it works any more than you need to know how USB works. You just need to know that it's the thing that makes Claude actually useful for your day-to-day.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

Here's your morning without MCP: You open Claude, type a question, get an answer, then go to Gmail to send the email, then go to your calendar to schedule the meeting, then go to your project management tool to update the task. Claude helped you think, but you did all the work.

Here's your morning with MCP: You tell Claude to check your email, draft responses to anything routine, schedule a meeting with the client who emailed about the project, and update your Asana board with the new timeline. Claude does all of it. You review the drafts, approve them, and move on with your day.

That's the difference. It's not about Claude being smarter. It's about Claude being able to reach into your tools and do things instead of just talking about them.

## What's Available Right Now

The main connections that work today:

**Communication:** Gmail (read, draft, search), Slack (read channels, send messages)

**Productivity:** Google Calendar (read and create events), Notion (read and update pages), Asana (manage tasks and projects)

**Development:** GitHub (repos, issues, PRs), Linear (issues, projects)

**Marketing and Sales:** HubSpot (contacts, deals, activities)

**Everything else:** Zapier MCP gives you a bridge to over 8,000 apps. If there's not a direct connection to your tool, there's probably a Zapier path.

New connectors are being added regularly. Six months from now this list will look different.

## What This Means For Your Business

If you're a solo operator or running a small team, MCP is the thing that turns Claude from "a helpful chatbot I occasionally ask questions" into "the assistant that handles my recurring tasks."

The business owners getting the most out of Claude right now aren't just asking it questions. They have it connected to their tools, running through their inboxes, managing their schedules, and processing their data. The setup takes time to get right, but once it's configured, it compounds. Every task you automate stays automated.

## The Honest Limitations

Some connectors work better than others. Email and calendar are solid. Some of the newer integrations are still rough. Things disconnect. Permissions can be finicky. It's a research preview in some areas, not a finished product.

You also need to understand that MCP lets Claude act on your behalf. That's powerful and it demands attention. You want to review what it's doing, especially early on. Don't give it access to everything on day one. Start with one or two connections, get comfortable with how it works, then expand.

And if you're on Windows, some of this isn't available to you yet. Cowork, which is the easiest way to use MCP as a non-developer, is macOS only for now.
