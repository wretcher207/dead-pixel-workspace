# Annie

David's girlfriend / ex-girlfriend. The role shifts constantly. Central figure in the [[writing-project]] — she gets more ink than anyone else.

## Profile

- Long curly blonde hair, blue eyes, very short (~4'4")
- Divorced, two kids (one with David)
- Presents as hippie-type, sweet disposition, high energy, fun
- David suspects bipolar disorder with antisocial personality disorder
- History of drug abuse
- Lost custody of first child after a police interaction she initiated backfired

## Behavioral Patterns

- **Die for the lie** — will never admit to anything regardless of evidence (from [[notes-annie-doesnt-drink]])
- **Persistence** — forces herself into David's life, preys on his sympathies
- **Hot/cold cycle** — begs to hang out, then sits in silence until sex
- **Deflection** — blames others (blames [[rory]] for her beer bottles)
- **Reassurance early on** — was genuinely good at sensing when David was pulling away and calming him down (from [[notes-meeting-annie]])

## Timeline

1. Met David on Instagram a few months after his divorce
2. First encounter by the railroad tracks in her gray Jeep Renegade
3. Seemed accepting of who David was — "that's okay"
4. Infidelity revealed (multiple instances)
5. Had a child with David
6. Called cops on David, ended up with a drug charge herself
7. David describes her as having "completely destroyed my life in the course of about 14 months"
8. Currently: David trying to cut ties, she keeps pulling back in

## Role in the Writing

Annie is the primary antagonist but David doesn't flatten her. He acknowledges the good parts were real. The writing about her is the sharpest and most sustained — she brings out his best prose because the emotional stakes are highest.
