# Cognitive Offloading

The practice of delegating mechanical and tedious work to AI while preserving human judgment for decisions that require taste, risk assessment, and discernment. Not replacement—liberation. The goal is freeing human bandwidth for the 10% of work that matters.

## The Core Insight

Human attention is zero-sum. Every minute spent on keystrokes, file organization, or initial synthesis is a minute not spent on strategy, taste, and judgment calls.

Traditional productivity advice says: be more efficient. Do both.

Cognitive offloading says: don't do the mechanical work at all. Delegate it. The constraint isn't your speed—it's your judgment capacity.

From [[article-6am-dispatch-parallel-workflows]], an orthopedic surgeon with a CEO role and content creation practice runs five parallel workflows before breakfast via [[claude-cowork]]. Not because he works faster, but because he dispatches tasks in five minutes and returns to finished work while doing something else. The AI isn't more efficient at bureaucracy—it's doing bureaucracy while he does thinking.

The pattern is: decide what matters, set up the workflow once, let Claude execute, review the output.

## The 6 AM Dispatch Pattern

[[article-6am-dispatch-parallel-workflows]] describes the practical implementation:

1. **Morning dispatch (5 minutes):** Define five workflows
   - Email triage and response drafting
   - Presentation preparation from existing slides
   - Content calendar planning with web research
   - Downloads folder organization
   - Deep research report on a chosen domain

2. **Morning coffee (while it runs):** You go about your day

3. **Return to deliverables (1 hour later):** Five mostly-finished projects requiring only review

The magic isn't that each task got done faster. It's that you didn't spend time narrating steps. You said "organize this" and came back to organization. No back-and-forth. No "wait, let me clarify"—because [[claude-cowork]] is autonomous.

From [[article-6am-dispatch-parallel-workflows]]: "Judgment calls—not keystrokes—are the scarce resource."

## What Gets Offloaded

**High-ROI offloading (tedious, rule-based, low judgment):**
- File organization and metadata
- Initial draft generation (first pass structure)
- Data extraction from documents or transcripts
- Email/message triage and response drafting
- Report generation from raw data
- Batch processing similar items
- Research synthesis and outlining
- Presentation scaffolding

**Low-ROI offloading (requires taste or taste-adjacent decisions):**
- Final design or creative output
- High-stakes communication
- Strategic decisions
- Pricing and resource allocation
- Client-facing final approval
- Anything that would require two rounds of revision anyway

The rule: delegate work that Claude can do correctly on first pass with minimal review.

## The Human Layer Remains

Crucial: this is not "set and forget." The human review step is non-negotiable.

From [[article-6am-dispatch-parallel-workflows]], all output requires human review. From [[article-cowork-10-use-cases-tested]], even successful workflows (bank reconciliation, content conversion) get checked for hallucinations and edge cases.

The offloading is efficient because the review is usually fast: spot-checking a drafted email takes 30 seconds instead of writing it taking five minutes.

## Relevance to David

As a solo operator with three domains (Dead Pixel Design development, Wretcher music production, writing), cognitive offloading directly addresses your constraint: you can't outsource the creative or judgment parts, but you can eliminate the bureaucratic overhead.

**For Dead Pixel:**
- Draft client proposals from templates (you review tone and scope)
- Organize project files by client and type (you verify correctness)
- Summarize client feedback across emails (you make decisions)
- Generate estimates from past projects (you adjust based on complexity)

**For Wretcher:**
- Organize recording sessions and stems (you review the structure)
- Summarize production notes and decisions (you use as a reference)
- Generate promotional copy (you refine the voice)
- Create social media content calendars (you approve themes)

**For writing:**
- Organize notes by character, scene, or theme (you ensure accuracy)
- Draft structural outlines from scattered notes (you shape the narrative)
- Generate dialogue variations (you pick the best)
- Manage continuity and consistency checks (you catch contradictions)

The pattern is consistent: offload the work that's deterministic and tedious, keep the work that requires your taste and judgment. The result is more finished work in the same amount of time, because you're not spending hours on scaffolding and organization.

## The Hidden Benefit

Cognitive offloading isn't just faster—it changes how you think. When you know Claude will handle the organization, you think differently about projects. You don't hesitate to start messy because you know the cleanup is automated. You don't avoid ambitious scope because you know the bureaucracy is handled.

This is the same effect as pen-and-paper journaling vs. journaling in a notebook: the medium changes the thinking.
