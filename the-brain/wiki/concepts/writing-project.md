# The Writing Project

David's autobiographical writing project. Raw, dark-humored, unflinching. Currently exists as a collection of essays/chapters and a character sheet. Building toward something structured — the exact form is still emerging.

## What Exists So Far

- **[[notes-cast-and-characters]]** — Dramatis personae. The cast with descriptions.
- **[[notes-writing-style]]** — Opening monologue / voice sample. Establishes tone and philosophy.
- **[[notes-meeting-annie]]** — Chapter draft. How David met [[annie]].
- **[[notes-annie-doesnt-drink]]** — Chapter draft. A sustained scene about Annie's secret drinking. The strongest standalone piece so far.

## Voice

- Direct address to the reader
- Profane but precise — every "fuck" carries weight
- Dark humor as structural element, not decoration
- Stream of consciousness with narrative discipline
- Self-aware but not self-pitying
- No filter, no softening, no protecting anyone

## Themes

- [[suffering-as-pathway]] — the philosophical backbone
- [[invisibility]] — the paradox of the unseen man telling all
- Generational patterns — addiction, incarceration, custody, repeating across [[jo]], [[dad]], [[david]]
- The absurdity of real life — funnier and darker than fiction
- Toxic love — [[annie]] as the primary case study, but not the only one
- Chosen family vs. blood — [[alyssa]] and [[lou]] vs. [[jo]] and [[dad]]

## Open Questions

> [!question] Structure
> Is this a linear memoir, a collection of essays, or something else? The pieces so far work both ways. The character sheet suggests a larger narrative; the standalone quality of "Annie Doesn't Drink" suggests essays.

> [!question] Scope
> How far back does this go? The current material covers post-divorce life. Does it reach back into childhood, the marriage, the straight edge days?
