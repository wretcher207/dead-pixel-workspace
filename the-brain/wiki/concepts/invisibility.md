# Invisibility

> "I try as hard as possible to be seen as little as possible. It's become a game at this point. How invisible can I make myself?"
> — [[david]], from [[notes-writing-style]]

## The Idea

David describes a deliberate effort to disappear — not depression-driven withdrawal but an active, almost competitive pursuit of going unnoticed. He's turned it into "a game." This is a coping mechanism, an identity, and a contradiction all at once.

## The Contradiction

The man who wants to be invisible is writing a memoir. He's building a business with his name on it ([[dead-pixel-design]]). He makes bong rip videos with careful shot composition (from [[notes-meeting-annie]]). He plays in a death metal band ([[wretcher]]).

This isn't hypocrisy — it's a tension that makes him interesting. He wants to control the terms of visibility. The memoir is on *his* terms. The videos are curated. The music is behind a stage name. Invisibility doesn't mean absence. It means choosing exactly when and how to be seen.

## Psychological Read

In Jungian terms, the invisible man is hiding from the collective — retreating into the personal. But the writing project is an eruption of the opposite impulse: a need to be witnessed. The game of invisibility may be what makes the writing possible. You can only tell the truth this raw if you've spent years learning how not to be seen.
