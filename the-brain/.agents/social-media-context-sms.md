## Target Audience

- **Primary audience**: Small business owners anywhere — solopreneurs, freelancers, small teams (1-20 people) who know they should be using AI but don't know where to start or got burned by ChatGPT and gave up
- **Pain points**: Overwhelmed by AI options, wasted time on tools that didn't deliver, can't afford a full-time developer or IT person, website looks like it was built in 2014, spending hours on tasks that could be automated
- **Goals**: Save time on repetitive work, look more professional online, stop losing money to inefficiency, actually understand what AI can do for their specific business
- **Sophistication level**: Beginner to intermediate. They use computers daily but "terminal" is a scary word. They need things explained in plain English, not jargon.
- **Where they hang out**: Facebook (local business groups), LinkedIn (B2B services), Reddit (r/smallbusiness, r/entrepreneur), YouTube/TikTok (tutorials, how-tos)

## Voice & Tone

- **Voice adjectives**: direct, practical, no-bullshit, slightly dark-humored, relatable
- **Formality**: Semi-casual — professional but sounds like a real person, not a marketing department
- **Humor**: Dry / occasional — earned through honesty, not forced
- **Phrases to use**: "here's what actually happens," "the part nobody tells you," "I tested this myself," "let me show you," "the short version is"
- **Phrases to avoid**: "leverage," "synergy," "unlock your potential," "game-changer," "in today's fast-paced world," "excited to announce," "thought leader," "digital transformation," any sentence that starts with "In an era where..."

## Anti-Patterns

- **Topics to avoid**: Partisan politics, competitor trash-talk (by name), personal health details, anything that reads like a get-rich-quick pitch
- **Tones to avoid**: Preachy, hype-y, motivational-poster, corporate buzzword soup, "rise and grind," condescending/explaining-down
- **Content types to avoid**: Engagement-bait, "tag someone who needs this," empty question posts, stock photo carousels, anything that could have been written by any agency for any client
