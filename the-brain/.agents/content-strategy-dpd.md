## Differentiation

**Voice positioning**: You are the guy who taught himself to build websites by talking to an AI — and now you set up that same AI for other business owners. Your content is the anti-agency pitch: no buzzwords, no inflated promises, just a developer in Maine showing you exactly what the tools do and whether they're worth your time. Your audience trusts you because you talk about what broke, not just what shipped.

**Content gaps to own**:
1. **MCP and Skills explained for non-developers** — Every existing article assumes the reader knows what a terminal is. Nobody is writing "here's what MCP means for your plumbing business."
2. **Honest Claude/Cowork testing with real small business tasks** — Most Cowork content is either hype or Reddit anecdotes. Nobody is systematically testing it against actual small business workflows and publishing the results.
3. **The self-taught-through-AI angle** — Most AI content creators are either developers writing for developers or marketers selling courses. Nobody is saying "I literally learned to code through this tool and now I build production sites with it."
4. **Price transparency in web development** — The industry thrives on opacity. Being direct about what things cost and why builds massive trust.
5. **Project Vend-style cautionary takes** — Everyone is selling AI as magic. Being the person who also says "here's where it fails" builds credibility that pure hype never will.

**Underserved audience segment**: Small business owners who tried ChatGPT, got mediocre results, and concluded "AI isn't for me." They're not anti-tech — they're burned. They need someone who validates that experience ("yeah, that happens") and shows them something that actually works for their specific situation. Nobody is specifically speaking to the AI-disappointed segment.

## Service Integration

Every piece of content should have a natural path to the Dead Pixel Design AI setup service:

- **Educational content** → "If you want this set up for your business, that's literally what I do."
- **Tool reviews** → "I configure these for clients — here's what I set up and why."
- **Behind the Build** → "This is the kind of work I do. Here's what it looks like."
- **Engagement posts** → Answer questions, demonstrate expertise, mention the service only when it's a genuine answer to someone's problem.

**CTA framework**: Soft, not aggressive. Never "BUY NOW." Always: "If you want help with this, I set this up for people." or "DM me if you want me to walk you through it." or just a link in bio/profile.
