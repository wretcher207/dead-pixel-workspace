## Reddit Post (r/smallbusiness)

**Title:** ChatGPT didn't work for my business. Then I stopped comparing it to Claude.

**Body:**

I see posts here all the time: "I tried ChatGPT and it was a waste of money." I get it. I thought the same thing six months ago.

But I was comparing apples to oranges.

ChatGPT is a really smart chatbot. Good for images. Good for looking up current news. If you use it the way everyone else does (ask a question, get an answer), it works fine. I still use it for those things.

Claude is built for different work entirely.

The difference hit me when I started working with documents. Like real business documents. A 100-page contract. Claude reads the whole thing and remembers what you said three sections ago. ChatGPT loses the thread around page 30. Same with market analysis or financial forecasts. Claude's output reads like someone actually thought about it.

But here's the real kicker: Claude's Cowork tool connects to your actual business infrastructure. Email, Slack, Asana, your spreadsheets. You can set up a recurring task: "Pull sales data from Stripe every Monday and drop it in our sheet with a summary." It runs every week on its own. ChatGPT can't do any of that.

Is it more expensive? Yes. The good features live in the $100/month plan. But if your business runs on recurring tasks and data that needs organizing, that tool pays for itself.

The trap I fell into: I used ChatGPT like everyone else uses ChatGPT. Then I was surprised it didn't move the needle.

The move: figure out what actually eats your time. The repetitive stuff, the data work, the things you keep meaning to automate. Build around that. ChatGPT for images. Claude if your business has actual workflows.

That's the difference between a tool and infrastructure.
