## 5. Reddit Post (r/smallbusiness or r/entrepreneur)

**Length:** 420 words

---

**Title:** "How I went from zero coding knowledge to building production websites (and what it actually took)"

---

I see a lot of posts here about people wanting to learn development but getting stuck on tutorials or online courses. I was that person. I tried it the traditional way: YouTube, courses, all that. It just didn't work for my brain.

So here's what I actually did, in case it helps someone.

**The setup that failed:** YouTube tutorials, Python courses, the works. I'd hit week three and lose interest because I was learning abstract concepts toward some hypothetical future project that didn't exist yet. I need to build something *real, right now*, and figure it out as I go. That's how I'm wired.

**The thing that worked:** I started using Claude Code (not ChatGPT. That's important). It's an AI that runs in your terminal, reads your files, writes code alongside you, explains what it's doing. First time I gave it a project brief and watched it scaffold a whole Next.js application with component structure and styling, something clicked. This wasn't a search engine. This was a collaborator.

**How the learning actually happened:** I didn't learn to code *and then* use Claude faster. I learned *by using* Claude. The cycle was: it writes something, I read it, I ask why, I break it, I rebuild it. Over and over. Like learning a language by having conversations instead of memorizing verb tables.

**The trap I fell into:** Early on, I'd accept whatever Claude generated without understanding it. Shipped it, client asked for changes, realized I had no idea why the code was structured that way. The fix was stupid simple: discipline. Read every function. Push back on every decision. "Why'd you structure it that way? Show me an alternative." Over time I went from "build me a navbar" to "implement navigation state like this."

**Why this matters:** Most people I talk to tried ChatGPT, got mediocre results, and wrote AI off entirely. I get it. But there's a difference between asking a chatbot questions and having AI actually integrated into your business workflows. The gap is setup. It's knowing what to ask for, how to structure it, what context to give.

That's actually harder than the learning part. Setup is configuration. Context. Real workflows. And that's what I do now at Dead Pixel. I help small business owners and entrepreneurs set up AI and development tools so they actually work for their situation, not some generic tutorial.

If you're stuck on the tutorial treadmill and want to know more, I'm happy to answer questions in the comments.
