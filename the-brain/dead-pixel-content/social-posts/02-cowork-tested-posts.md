I see a lot of AI hype on this sub. Most of it is overblown. So I tested Claude Cowork (the new desktop app) against actual work instead of hypotheticals.

Cowork excels at mechanical work: sorting, drafting, organizing, categorizing. One user reported doing 49 hours of work in about an hour. It's terrible at decisions. Don't ask it to price your services or decide whether to fire a vendor.

If you spend real hours on email, filing, reporting, or data entry (especially if you've tried ChatGPT and bounced), yes. This is a different tool.
