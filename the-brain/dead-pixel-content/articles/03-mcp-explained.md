# MCP Explained Like You're a Business Owner, Not a Developer

You keep hearing the letters MCP. Maybe in an article about AI. Maybe from someone trying to sell you something. Nobody explains it in a way that connects to your business.

MCP stands for Model Context Protocol. The name is useless. Here's what matters: it lets Claude reach into your tools and actually do things.

Without MCP, Claude is locked in a box. It can think. It can write. It can analyze. But it can't touch anything in your world. No email. No calendar. No CRM. You copy and paste everything back and forth like it's 2009.

With MCP, Claude plugs directly into what you're already using. Gmail. Slack. Notion. Asana. Google Calendar. HubSpot. Through Zapier, you get over 8,000 more apps. Think of MCP like USB for AI. A standard connection that lets Claude work with whatever you've got.

## Here's what actually changes

Without MCP: You tell Claude something. Claude tells you something back. You go to Gmail to send the email. Then to your calendar to schedule the meeting. Then to your project tool to log the task. Claude gave you ideas. You did the work.

With MCP: You tell Claude to check your email, draft the routine responses, schedule the meeting, and update Asana. Claude does all of it. You review. You approve. You move on.

That's the difference. Not that Claude gets smarter. That Claude gets to actually reach into your business and move things around instead of just talking about them.

## What's connected right now

**Communication:** Gmail (read, draft, search), Slack (read channels, send messages)

**Productivity:** Google Calendar, Notion, Asana

**Development:** GitHub, Linear

**Sales:** HubSpot

**Everything else:** Zapier MCP bridges to 8,000+ apps.

New tools get added regularly. In six months this list will look different. That's the whole point.

## What this looks like for your business

If you're running a small operation, MCP is what turns Claude from "a chatbot I ask questions" into "the thing that handles my recurring tasks."

The business owners getting real value aren't just asking Claude questions. They have it connected to their tools. Running through their inbox. Managing their calendar. Processing their data. Once you set it up right, it compounds. Every task you automate stays automated.

## The real limitations

Some connectors work better than others. Email and calendar are solid. Some newer integrations are still rough around the edges. Things disconnect. Permissions get finicky. It's not a finished product yet in some areas.

More important: MCP lets Claude act on your behalf. That's powerful. It demands attention. Don't give it access to everything on day one. Start with one connection. Get comfortable. Then expand.
