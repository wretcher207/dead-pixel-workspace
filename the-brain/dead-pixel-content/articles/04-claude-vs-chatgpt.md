# Claude vs ChatGPT for Small Business: An Honest Comparison From Someone Who Uses Both

I use both of these tools every day. Not as a hobby. Not in a sandbox. Real work. The kind where if it breaks, something breaks.

Most people never get far enough with either tool to know the difference. They chat with one, get confused when it hallucinates, and move on. That's not what I'm talking about.

## The Real Difference

Claude and ChatGPT aren't the same thing wearing different hats. They're built by different companies with different philosophies about what AI should actually do.

ChatGPT is the generalist. It does a dozen things: chat, image generation, web search, plugins. Good at some, mediocre at others. The jack-of-all-trades problem.

Claude is the specialist. Built for serious work. Long documents. Deep analysis. And this is the key: it has tools that actually do things on your computer instead of just talking about doing them.

## When Claude Gets the Work Done

**Big documents.** Seriously. If you're dealing with a 100-page contract, a financial forecast, a research database, Claude holds it all in context without losing the thread. ChatGPT gets lost around page thirty. You end up repeating yourself or getting generic answers. Claude remembers what you said three sections ago.

**Writing that needs to think.** Market analysis. Research summaries. Proposals that need spine. Claude's output reads like someone worked on it. ChatGPT often sounds like it was written by a committee of robots.

**Actually doing things.** This is huge and most people miss it. Claude's Cowork tool connects to your email, files, calendar, Slack, Asana, Notion. You can tell Claude to organize your inbox or pull data from a spreadsheet or write a report from your email archives. It doesn't just talk about it. It does it. ChatGPT can't touch any of that.

**Automation you can rely on.** You can build recurring tasks. "Every Monday morning, pull sales data from Stripe and drop it in my spreadsheet with a summary." Claude does it. You set it up once. It keeps working. ChatGPT doesn't have this.

## Where ChatGPT Actually Wins

**Images.** It can make them. Claude can't. If you need product mockups, social graphics, anything visual, ChatGPT has you covered. Claude doesn't.

**Current events.** ChatGPT browses the web and fetches news. Faster. Simpler. Claude can do it but it's an extra step.

**Name recognition.** Everyone knows ChatGPT. If you're explaining AI to a client who's never used it, ChatGPT is the reference point. Silly reason to pick a tool, but it matters in sales conversations.

**Lower price floor.** $20 a month gets you ChatGPT Plus. Claude's powerful features live in the $100 plan. If you're broke, ChatGPT is cheaper.

## The Thing Nobody Wants to Hear

Most small business owners fail with AI the same way they fail with tools in general: they don't set them up for their actual work. They grab a tool, use it like everyone else, and wonder why it doesn't move the needle.

You tried ChatGPT six months ago. It felt slow. It hallucinated. You decided AI wasn't ready. What really happened: you didn't build anything into it. You used it like a search engine with a weird personality.

Claude is better when you actually configure it for your business. Connect it to your tools. Give it access to your actual data. Build workflows around what you actually do. Then it's not a chatbot. It's an employee.

## My Recommendation

If you're starting from zero and you want AI to handle actual work, Claude is the better bet. Cowork costs money, but it's the only tool that reaches into your business and does stuff.

Keep ChatGPT for images. That's what it's genuinely best at.
