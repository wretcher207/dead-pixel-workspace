# Why Most Small Business Websites Fail

Your website looks like everyone else's. And that's the problem.

I see it all the time. A small business owner needs a web presence, so they buy a template. Could be Wix, could be Squarespace, could be a WordPress theme off ThemeForest. Doesn't matter which. They all look the same. The same fonts, the same layout patterns, the same stock photos of smiling people in business casual.

Then they add their logo and call it done.

Here's the thing: the template isn't the problem. The technology is fine. Responsive design works. Hosting is reliable. The actual problem is that nobody thought about *experience*. What does someone feel when they land on your site? Do they immediately understand what you do? Does it feel like *you*, or does it feel like a placeholder?

Most websites fail because they're forgettable. Not because they break. Because they don't stick.

When I build a site for a client, I'm not just moving text into a template. I'm building something that has weight. Something that feels intentional. That means everything from copy that sounds like a real person to design choices that reflect what the business actually does. It means considering what someone landing cold on your homepage needs to know in the first three seconds.

A template can't do that. A template is made for everyone, which means it's made for no one.

The businesses that stand out online are the ones where someone actually thought about the experience. Where the site feels like it was built for specific people solving a specific problem, not just broadcast to the void.

If your site looks like every other site, you're not broken. You're invisible.
