Build a **production-ready multi-section cafe website** for a southern Maine cafe called **Nutty Netties**.

This site should feel **cute, memorable, warm, and slightly eccentric**, but never childish, loud, or gimmicky. It should avoid the generic “rustic hipster coffee shop” look and instead create a distinct brand presence that feels like a beloved local spot with a playful personality.

## Core Brand Concept

Nutty Netties should feel like:

* a cozy neighborhood cafe with a wink
* a little offbeat in a charming way
* storybook warmth mixed with polished modern web design
* handmade, thoughtful, and full of small delightful details

The mood is:
**whimsical but tasteful**
**cozy but art directed**
**playful but commercially viable**
**cute without becoming cartoonish**

This is a spec website, so the design must feel convincing, premium, and portfolio-worthy.

## Visual Identity Direction

Create a visual language inspired by:

* recipe cards
* handwritten margin notes
* warm coffeehouse textures
* subtle woodland charm
* small-town Maine coziness
* bakery display cases, vintage paper labels, cafe chalkboards, jam jar labels, pie tins, and morning light through windows

Do **not** make it look like:

* a generic Squarespace cafe template
* an overdone farmhouse aesthetic
* a children’s brand
* a maximalist cartoon world
* a trendy ultra-minimal luxury brand
* a kitschy diner
* a generic coffee startup

## Color Palette

Use a palette that feels edible and warm:

* steamed milk cream
* soft oat beige
* espresso brown
* muted pistachio or sage green
* dusty berry or jam red as an accent
* occasional buttery gold highlights

Keep contrast accessible, but maintain softness. The palette should feel warm, baked, and welcoming.

## Typography

Use a typography system with personality:

* a soft editorial serif for headlines
* a clean, highly readable sans serif for body copy
* a restrained handwritten or note-style accent font for tiny annotations, labels, or microcopy moments only

Typography should feel tactile and intentional. Headlines should feel distinctive and ownable. Body text must remain highly legible and production-ready.

## Mascot / Signature Motif

Incorporate a **subtle illustrated squirrel motif** as a recurring brand element, but keep it tasteful and minimal.
This squirrel should not feel like a cartoon mascot. It should feel like:

* a small illustrated emblem
* a hand-drawn signature
* a recurring icon hidden throughout the experience
* a visual joke used sparingly

Possible uses:

* perched near section dividers
* tiny loading or hover detail
* a small “Nettie note” label next to featured menu items
* hidden easter egg interaction

The squirrel should feel like part of the identity, not the center of the identity.

## Art Direction

Photography or generated visuals should emphasize:

* warm natural light
* cozy interior details
* pastries, coffee, ceramic mugs, handwritten menus
* intimate, inviting framing
* subtle visual storytelling
* textures like paper grain, linen, ceramic glaze, wood, and frosted glass

Use layered depth, soft shadows, and tactile surfaces. The site should feel almost touchable.

## Overall UX Goal

The site should feel like browsing a cafe menu and bulletin board inside a charming local spot, but translated into a polished premium web experience.

The user should feel:

* welcomed immediately
* curious to keep exploring
* subtly delighted by tiny details
* confident this is a real, loved business

## Site Structure

Design and build a complete homepage with the following sections:

### 1. Hero Section

Create a striking but warm hero section.
Include:

* a strong branded headline area
* supporting text
* clear primary and secondary CTA buttons
* layered cafe imagery or illustration
* subtle motion, such as drifting steam, floating paper labels, or light parallax

The hero should immediately communicate that Nutty Netties is not a generic cafe.

Possible tone for copy:

* playful
* self-aware
* warm
* local
* lightly quirky

Avoid cringey jokes or overdone puns.

### 2. Featured Specials / Signature Items

Present featured drinks and pastries in a visually unique way.
Use a concept like:

* recipe cards
* pinned note cards
* menu tags
* slightly rotated paper cards on a tabletop
* bakery labels in a display case

Cards should have:

* item name
* short description
* price
* optional little annotation or “Nettie note”

Hover interactions should feel delightful and tactile.

### 3. About Section

Create a charming about section that feels more personal than corporate.
Consider a layout inspired by:

* a storybook page
* a handwritten kitchen note
* a framed note on a cafe wall

This section should tell the story of Nutty Netties as a beloved local place with character and warmth.

### 4. Full Menu Preview

Include a menu section with categories like:

* coffee
* tea
* breakfast
* pastries
* lunch
* seasonal specials

This should not feel like a boring restaurant PDF dump. It should feel curated, readable, and visually integrated into the site brand.

### 5. Atmosphere / Gallery Section

Show the cafe’s mood through imagery or stylized composition.
This section should sell the feeling of visiting:

* morning light
* cozy corners
* pastries under glass
* plants, mugs, wood, paper, baked goods
* southern Maine local charm

### 6. Visit / Hours / Location Section

Include clear practical information:

* hours
* address
* map or map placeholder
* parking note
* dine-in / takeout
* social links

This section should still feel branded and charming, not like a generic footer block.

### 7. Footer

Include:

* logo or wordmark
* navigation
* contact info
* social links
* tiny charming sign-off line

## Interaction Design

Add subtle, polished, premium motion throughout.
Examples:

* steam drifting from a coffee cup
* buttons with a soft press or squish effect
* cards straightening slightly on hover
* tiny paper shuffle effects
* cursor-adjacent crumb or dust motes
* parallax layers in hero imagery
* section reveals that feel like a page turning or note sliding into place

Motion must remain restrained and elegant. No over-animation.

## UI Components

Create reusable branded components:

* CTA buttons
* menu cards
* section headers
* price tags
* annotation badges
* testimonial cards if needed
* icon system with organic, hand-touched feel

All components should feel cohesive and custom.

## Layout & Responsiveness

The site must be fully production-ready and responsive.
Requirements:

* desktop-first elegance with strong mobile adaptation
* clear spacing system
* visual hierarchy that feels editorial but accessible
* responsive stacking for cards and feature layouts
* excellent tap targets on mobile
* menu content remains readable on small screens
* animations should degrade gracefully

## Build Requirements

Generate this as a **real website design and implementation**, not a concept art mockup.
The output should be:

* polished
* believable
* portfolio quality
* fully structured for front-end build
* easy to adapt with real business content later

Use best practices for:

* accessibility
* semantic structure
* responsive behavior
* performance-conscious animation
* clean component architecture
