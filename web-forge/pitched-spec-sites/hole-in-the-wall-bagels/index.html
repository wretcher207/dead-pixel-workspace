A bagel shop on Main

A small room,
a big window,
a warm oven.

We've been baking bagels on Rockland's Main Street since 2018, in a sunlit corner of the Good Tern Co-op with turquoise window frames and the harbor a block away. The dough is mixed the day before. Every morning it gets rolled by hand, boiled, and baked in small batches until the racks are full.

Come in before 9am for the full board. Grab a coffee. Stay a while, the light is good in here.

What we bake

Four corners of the board.

A short list done well. These four run every day we're open. Sesame, everything, poppy, plain — plus whatever specials the baker feels like pulling that morning.

— N° 01

Plain

The one we're proudest of. Crackling crust, chewy pull, a little malt. This is the bagel we argue about at 5am.

— N° 02

Sesame

Heavily seeded top and bottom, toasted in the oven. Goes well with plain cream cheese and an aggressive amount of it.

— N° 03

Everything

Our own blend of garlic, onion, poppy, sesame, caraway and flake salt. Hold the napkin, embrace the seeds on your shirt.

— N° 04

Poppy

Slightly sweet, heavy with poppy seed, the kind of bagel that goes quiet with butter and a cup of hot coffee.

The shop

Morning light, a hot oven, the harbor a block away.

Community-owned

Baked by the
neighborhood,
for the neighborhood.

Hole In The Wall is part of the Good Tern Co-op — a member-owned grocery rooted in Rockland since 1981. When we reopened the bagel shop in 2018, we kept it simple: good ingredients, local hands, whatever fits in the oven that day.

If you bought a bagel here this morning, you helped a co-op pay bakers, buy flour from Maine Grains, and keep a small room on Main Street open for everybody.
