# Google Stitch Prompts — Ghostly Engraving LLC

---

## 1. HOMEPAGE

Design a full-length homepage for a premium custom engraving business called Ghostly Engraving LLC. This is a boutique laser engraving and personalized gift brand based in Maine. The site should feel like a high-end artisan brand, not a generic Shopify template.

### Layout Structure (top to bottom)

**Navigation bar:** Sticky, translucent dark navbar with a frosted glass effect. Logo (ghost-dog mascot in white) on the left, nav links center-right (Shop, Custom Orders, Business Gifts, Gallery, About, Contact). A soft purple "Request a Quote" button on the far right. Subtle bottom border glow in pale violet.

**Hero section:** Full-viewport hero. Dark background transitioning from deep midnight navy at the top to a rich celestial purple toward the bottom, with a faint cosmic dust texture and soft scattered star particles. A single hero product image — an engraved tumbler or cutting board — sits off-center right, dramatically lit from the left with a warm amber sidelight and cool purple ambient fill. The product rests on a dark slate or matte black surface, with a faint reflection beneath it. Headline left-aligned in an elegant serif typeface (like Playfair Display or Cormorant Garant), large and stacked: "Precision Engraving. Personal Touch." Subtext in a clean sans-serif (Inter or DM Sans), lighter weight: "Custom-crafted keepsakes, gifts, and branded pieces — made in Maine." Below, two buttons side by side: a primary filled button ("Shop Collections") in soft violet with white text, and a secondary ghost button ("Start a Custom Order") with a thin violet border. The ghost-dog mascot appears as a very subtle watermark at about 5% opacity in the lower-right background area.

**Featured Collections row:** Horizontal scroll on mobile, 3-across grid on desktop. Each card is a tall rectangle with rounded corners, a dark matte background, and a single product photo centered inside with generous padding. Product photos should feel editorially styled — moody lighting, dark surfaces, shallow depth of field. Categories: Drinkware, Cutting Boards, Custom Gifts. Each card has a category name in elegant small caps beneath the image and a subtle hover glow (soft purple outer shadow) on desktop.

**Why Ghostly Engraving section:** Three-column feature grid on a slightly lighter dark background (charcoal). Each column has a thin-line icon at top (line weight ~1.5px, in soft lilac), a short bold heading, and two lines of body text. Features: "Precision Laser Engraving" / "Designed for You" / "Crafted in Maine." Icons should feel minimal and refined — not clipart. Think architectural line drawings.

**Custom Order Process section:** Stepped horizontal timeline layout. Four steps in a row with connecting lines between them. Each step is a numbered circle (soft purple fill, white number) with a label beneath. Steps: Tell Us Your Vision → Review Your Proof → We Craft It → Delivered to You. Below the timeline, a centered CTA: "Start Your Custom Order" in a wide pill-shaped button, soft purple with a gentle glow.

**Best Sellers grid:** 2x3 product grid on desktop, vertical scroll on mobile. Product cards have a dark card background, product image with a subtle vignette, product name, short description, and a "View Details" link in soft violet. Products feel gift-ready — imagine them on dark velvet or slate surfaces with moody directional lighting.

**Occasions / Use Cases section:** Full-width section with a deep purple-to-black gradient background. A row of circular image thumbnails, each representing an occasion: Weddings, Birthdays, Memorials, Corporate Events, Holidays, Just Because. Each circle has a thin glowing purple ring around it. Label text beneath in small caps.

**Testimonial section:** Single testimonial in a large pull-quote style. Oversized opening quotation mark in soft violet. Quote text in an italic serif. Customer name and location beneath in small sans-serif. Left-right arrows for navigation. Background is a very subtle cosmic grain texture.

**Business Gifting callout:** Split section — left side is a lifestyle image of branded tumblers (stacked, logo-engraved, professionally lit), right side has a headline: "Custom Gifts for Your Business" with supporting text about bulk orders, client gifts, employee recognition. CTA button: "Learn More" in the standard soft violet style.

**Footer:** Dark background, near-black. Four columns: Brand (logo + short tagline + social icons), Quick Links, Contact Info (Lewiston, ME / email / phone), and a mini email signup form. Ghost-dog mascot as a small subtle watermark. Copyright bar at bottom.

### Visual Direction

**Color palette:** Primary background: deep midnight (#0B0D17). Card surfaces: dark charcoal (#161822). Accent: soft celestial violet (#9B7FD4). Secondary accent: pale lilac (#C4B0E8). Text: warm white (#F0ECF5). Highlight glow: pale purple at low opacity for hover states and borders.

**Typography:** Headlines in a refined serif with generous letter-spacing (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garant, or similar). Body text in a clean geometric sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans, or similar). All type should feel spacious — generous line height, intentional whitespace.

**Lighting and mood:** Product photography direction is dark, moody, warm sidelight with cool ambient fill. Think whiskey brand photography or premium watch advertising. Products should glow against darkness. Surfaces beneath products: dark slate, matte black, raw wood, dark marble.

**Materials and textures:** Subtle cosmic grain/noise overlay at very low opacity across dark sections. Faint star particle effect in the hero (not animated in the design, but placed as if frozen). Frosted glass effect on navbar. Matte dark card surfaces that feel physical. No glossy or plasticky surfaces.

**Iconography:** Thin-line icons, 1.5px stroke weight, in lilac. Minimal and geometric. No filled icons, no cartoon style, no emoji.

**Interaction and motion suggestions:** Gentle fade-up on scroll for sections. Product cards scale slightly (1.02) on hover with a purple outer glow. Buttons have a subtle glow pulse on hover. Navbar becomes more opaque on scroll. Smooth transitions everywhere — nothing abrupt.

**What to avoid:** No bright white backgrounds. No neon colors. No Halloween imagery (bats, cobwebs, jack-o-lanterns). No cluttered grid layouts. No generic stock photography. No clip art. No flat/lifeless product images. No template-feeling hero banners with text overlaid on a busy photo. No Comic Sans, novelty fonts, or overly decorative type. The ghost mascot should never feel cartoonish — it's used as a refined brand signature, like a maker's mark.

## 2. CUSTOM ORDERS PAGE

Design a dedicated custom orders page for Ghostly Engraving LLC. This is the primary conversion page for customers who want personalized, one-of-a-kind engraved products. It should feel premium, reassuring, and frictionless. The page should make custom ordering feel simple and exciting, not intimidating.

### Layout Structure (top to bottom)

**Page hero:** Shorter than the homepage hero — roughly 40vh. Deep midnight-to-purple gradient background with faint cosmic dust. A headline in elegant serif: "Your Vision. Our Craft." Subline in clean sans-serif: "From a single personalized gift to a full custom run — we bring your ideas to life in wood, metal, acrylic, and more." Below, a single CTA button: "Get a Free Quote." On the right side or slightly behind the text, a beautifully lit product composition — two or three engraved items at different angles (a tumbler, a cutting board, and a keychain or ornament), overlapping slightly, on a dark surface with soft directional lighting. The ghost-dog mascot is faintly visible as a watermark in the background.

**How It Works section:** Full-width section with a very dark charcoal background. A bold heading centered: "How Custom Orders Work." Below, four horizontal steps in a card-based layout. Each card is a dark rounded rectangle with a number badge (small circle, violet fill, white number) in the upper-left corner. Each card contains a step illustration — a minimal line-art icon in lilac — a bold step title, and a short description. Steps: (1) Share Your Idea — "Tell us what you're looking for. Upload a logo, sketch, or just describe your vision." (2) Review Your Proof — "We'll send you a digital mockup before anything gets engraved." (3) We Craft It — "Your piece is laser engraved with precision and care in our Maine workshop." (4) Receive Your Order — "Carefully packaged and shipped directly to you." Cards sit in a single row on desktop, stacked vertically on mobile.

**What Can Be Personalized section:** Two-column layout. Left column: a vertical list of material categories, each as a row with a thin-line icon and label. Materials: Wood, Metal, Acrylic, Glass, Slate, Leather. Right column: a large, moody product photograph that changes contextually (in the static design, show a beautifully lit engraved wooden piece). The whole section sits on a dark background with a faint purple gradient glow on the left edge.

**Product Category Examples section:** A horizontal card row — four to five cards showing different custom order types. Each card is a tall dark rectangle with a single product image filling the top two-thirds and a category label at the bottom. Categories: Personalized Gifts, Wedding & Event Pieces, Memorial & Keepsake Items, Business Branding, Home Décor. Photography should be dark and dramatic — products lit against darkness, on natural surfaces. Cards have a subtle purple border-glow on hover.

**File & Artwork Guidelines section:** Clean, informational section on a slightly lighter dark background. Small heading: "Preparing Your Artwork." A two-column layout: left column lists accepted file types with small icons (SVG, PNG, AI, PDF, JPG), right column has brief guidance text about resolution, vector vs. raster, and a reassuring note: "Not sure about your file? Send it anyway — we'll work with you." Below both columns, a single "Upload Your Files" button in soft violet, full width on mobile.

**Pricing Transparency section:** A simple centered text block. Heading: "Fair, Transparent Pricing." Body text explaining that pricing depends on material, size, complexity, and quantity — with a note that they provide a free quote before any work begins. No hidden fees messaging. A subtle horizontal line separator with a small ghost-dog icon centered on it, acting as a section divider.

**FAQ Accordion:** Dark background. A heading: "Common Questions." Below, five or six expandable FAQ rows. Each row is a dark card with a question in bold sans-serif and a small plus/chevron icon on the right. When expanded, the answer text appears beneath in lighter weight. Questions should cover: turnaround time, minimum orders, bulk pricing, proofing process, return policy, file requirements. The active/expanded row has a soft left-border accent in violet.

**Quote Request Form section:** Full-width section with a very dark background and a subtle radial purple glow emanating from the center. Heading: "Start Your Custom Order." Subtext: "Tell us about your project and we'll get back to you within 24 hours." The form sits in a centered dark card with rounded corners and a thin violet border. Fields: Name, Email, Phone (optional), Project Type (dropdown: Personal Gift, Wedding/Event, Business/Bulk, Memorial, Other), Material Preference (dropdown), Quantity Estimate, Project Description (textarea), File Upload button. A large submit button at the bottom: "Request Your Free Quote" in filled violet. The form should feel spacious — generous field padding, clean labels, no visual clutter.

**Trust bar:** A slim horizontal strip near the bottom. Three trust signals in a row with thin-line icons: "100% Satisfaction" / "Free Digital Proof" / "Handcrafted in Maine." Small text, subtle lilac icons, dark background.

### Visual Direction

**Color palette:** Same as homepage — deep midnight, dark charcoal surfaces, soft celestial violet accents, pale lilac for secondary elements, warm white text.

**Typography:** Same hierarchy as homepage. Page title in large serif. Section headings in medium serif. Body text, form labels, and FAQ text in clean sans-serif. Step numbers and badges in sans-serif bold.

**Lighting and mood:** All product photography on this page should feel intimate and craftsmanlike. Think close-up detail shots showing engraving texture, wood grain, the precision of the laser work. Shallow depth of field. Warm amber key light with cool purple fill. Dark, moody, premium.

**Materials and textures:** Faint cosmic grain overlay. Dark card surfaces with subtle inner shadow to create depth. The form area has a radial violet glow in the background that feels like a soft spotlight. Section dividers use thin lines or the ghost-dog silhouette as a decorative break.

**Iconography:** Consistent thin-line style (1.5px, lilac). Icons for process steps should be simple and symbolic: a speech bubble, a pencil/proof, a laser beam, a package. Material icons: a wood grain symbol, a metal sheet, an acrylic block, etc.

**Interaction and motion suggestions:** FAQ rows expand smoothly with a slide-down animation. Form fields have a subtle violet border-glow on focus. Process step cards fade up sequentially on scroll. Category cards have a slight scale and glow on hover. The file upload button pulses gently.

**What to avoid:** No overwhelming form fields — keep it simple and inviting. No generic clip art for process steps. No bright white sections breaking the dark palette. No stock photography of people shaking hands or pointing at screens. No "contact us for pricing" vagueness without explanation. No overwhelming walls of text — keep copy tight and scannable.

## 3. BUSINESS GIFTING PAGE

Design a dedicated business gifting and wholesale page for Ghostly Engraving LLC. This page targets small businesses, realtors, event planners, and organizations looking for branded custom-engraved products. It should feel polished and professional enough to win B2B clients, while maintaining the brand's premium artisan identity. This is positioned as a revenue growth engine — the page should make business clients feel like Ghostly Engraving is the obvious local partner for custom branded gifts.

### Layout Structure (top to bottom)

**Page hero:** 40-50vh hero with a deep midnight gradient. On the left, a bold serif headline: "Custom Branded Gifts That Actually Get Kept." Subline in clean sans-serif: "Engraved drinkware, desk accessories, and branded keepsakes for your team, your clients, and your next event." CTA button: "Request a Bulk Quote." On the right, a product composition — a stack or group of branded tumblers (like the eagle-logo tumblers from their actual product line) arranged in a visually striking pyramid or diagonal arrangement, dramatically lit with warm sidelight on a dark surface. The image should immediately communicate volume and quality.

**Who This Is For section:** A four-column grid on desktop (2x2 on tablet, stacked on mobile). Each cell is a dark card with a thin-line icon at top, a bold label, and one line of description. Categories: Real Estate Agents ("Closing gifts your clients will actually remember"), Small Businesses ("Branded products that represent your quality"), Event Organizers ("Custom giveaways and awards for any occasion"), Corporate Teams ("Employee gifts and recognition pieces that feel personal"). Each card has a thin top-border accent in soft violet.

**Product Showcase for Business section:** A horizontal scrolling gallery or a staggered grid of product photos. Each image shows a business-oriented engraved product: a desk nameplate, a set of branded tumblers, an engraved bottle opener with a company logo, a custom award plaque, branded coasters. Photography should feel editorial — moody, dark backgrounds, warm directional light. Products are shown in context where possible (on a desk, at an event table). Each image has a small label overlay at the bottom with the product type.

**Why Partner With Ghostly section:** Split layout — left side is a moody atmospheric image (the workshop, a laser in action, or a close-up of engraving detail), right side has three value propositions stacked vertically, each with a thin-line icon and short text. Value props: "No Minimums on Most Items" / "Free Digital Proofs Before Production" / "Fast Turnaround for Rush Orders." Below the value props, a small note: "Based in Lewiston, Maine — serving businesses across New England."

**Bulk Pricing section:** A clean, minimal pricing table or tier card layout. Three tier cards side by side: "Small Run" (1-24 pieces), "Standard" (25-99 pieces), "Volume" (100+ pieces). Each card is a dark rounded rectangle with the tier name at top, quantity range, and key benefits (like "per-unit pricing decreases," "dedicated project manager," "priority production"). The middle card ("Standard") is slightly elevated or highlighted with a violet border to suggest it's the most popular. Below the cards, a note: "Every order starts with a free custom quote."

**Use Cases / Occasions grid:** A 3x2 grid of dark cards, each representing a business gifting occasion. Each card has a small moody background image (blurred or at low opacity) with a centered text label. Occasions: Client Appreciation, Employee Onboarding, Holiday Gifts, Trade Shows & Conferences, Awards & Recognition, Grand Openings. Cards have a subtle dark overlay with text in warm white. On hover, the background image becomes slightly more visible and a soft purple glow appears at the card edges.

**Testimonial / Social Proof section:** A wider testimonial card layout — one or two large quote cards side by side. Each card has a large pull-quote in italic serif, attribution beneath (name, business, location), and a small star rating. Background is dark with a faint purple radial glow. If possible, include a small "100% Recommend — 7 Reviews" trust badge pulled from their actual review data.

**Bulk Order Inquiry Form:** Full-width dark section with a centered form card. Heading: "Let's Build Something for Your Brand." Subline: "Tell us about your project and we'll get back to you with a custom quote." Form fields: Business Name, Contact Name, Email, Phone, Project Type (dropdown: Client Gifts, Employee Gifts, Event Items, Promotional Products, Branded Merchandise, Other), Estimated Quantity, Timeline (dropdown: No Rush, 2-4 Weeks, Under 2 Weeks), Project Details (textarea), Logo Upload button. Submit button: "Request Your Bulk Quote" in filled violet. The form card has a thin violet border and sits on a dark background with a subtle radial glow.

**Partner CTA strip:** A slim full-width section near the bottom with a slightly more vibrant purple gradient background. Centered text: "Ready to make your brand unforgettable?" with a white CTA button: "Get Started Today." This acts as a final conversion nudge before the footer.

### Visual Direction

**Color palette:** Same core palette as the rest of the site. Deep midnight backgrounds, dark charcoal card surfaces, soft celestial violet accents. On this page, allow the violet to be used slightly more generously — this is a sales-forward page, so the accent color can work harder. The pricing tier highlight and the final CTA strip can push the purple a bit further.

**Typography:** Headlines in the brand serif typeface — confident and large. Subheadings in bold sans-serif. Body text in regular-weight sans-serif. Pricing card details in a slightly condensed or tabular sans-serif for clarity. The pull-quote testimonials in an italic serif.

**Lighting and mood:** Product photography should feel professional and intentional. Imagine a branding agency's portfolio shots — products arranged with care on dark matte surfaces, dramatic but not theatrical lighting. For the business audience, the imagery should whisper "quality" and "reliability." Close-ups of engraving detail communicate precision. The group/stack shots of branded tumblers communicate volume capability.

**Materials and textures:** Same cosmic grain overlay. Pricing tier cards should feel slightly more tactile — a very faint inner shadow or slight bevel to suggest dimensionality. The partner CTA strip can introduce a slightly more saturated violet gradient to stand out from the rest of the page.

**Iconography:** Same thin-line icon system (1.5px, lilac). For this page, icons should lean toward business concepts: a handshake, a gift box, a clock/timer, a certificate/award, a storefront, a calendar.

**Interaction and motion suggestions:** Pricing tier cards have a gentle lift effect on hover (slight translateY and shadow increase). Use-case cards reveal their background image more fully on hover. Form fields glow violet on focus. The page should feel energetic but controlled — slightly more interactive than the homepage to encourage engagement from business buyers who are evaluating the service.

**What to avoid:** No generic B2B stock photography (handshakes in front of whiteboards, people pointing at laptops). No overly corporate design language that breaks from the brand. No aggressive sales copy ("ACT NOW," "LIMITED TIME"). No pricing that looks like a SaaS plan page — keep it artisan and personal. No cluttered product grids that feel like a catalog. The page should feel like a premium pitch deck, not a wholesale order form.

## General Notes for All Stitch Prompts

- The ghost-dog mascot from the logo should appear as a refined watermark or maker's mark — never as a cartoon character. Use it at 3-8% opacity in backgrounds or as a small silhouette in section dividers.
- All product photography direction: dark, moody, warm-lit, on natural surfaces (dark wood, slate, matte black, dark marble). Shallow depth of field. The products should feel like they belong in a premium gift guide or design magazine.
- The celestial/cosmic texture (stars, dust, grain) should be ambient and environmental — never foreground. It creates atmosphere, not theme.
- Every page should feel like it breathes. Generous section padding (120-160px between major sections on desktop). Wide margins. No cramping.
- The site should feel like a refined independent brand — not a marketplace, not a template store, not a hobby shop. Every design choice should reinforce the perception: "These people take their craft seriously."
